Weekly E-Mail Briefings ✉

70% of the examination in this module consists of a case study. In addition, there are 2 x 15% in the form of short multiple choice tests (online). The case study is divided into weekly tasks, for which we learn the necessary concepts and tools week by week. To know exactly which tasks are due and which contents are relevant, you will receive a short email briefing once a week. These briefings contain the following information:

◼ What materials should you work with?

Showing up is mandatory

Participation is mandatory for our Monday sessions via Zoom. If you can't make it, please notify me via MS Teams (s. below) with a short explanation. It's OK to be absent, but this should not happen too often.

Please do not miss a session unexcused! The presentation of the results of the case study is an essential grading criterion. If you are not there I am likely to pick you to present.

For the individual Friday dates, which can be booked by everyone as needed, the obligation to participate does not apply.

Always come prepared

In this module, you will prepare exercises every week and present your solutions in our sessions every now and then. We reserve a large part of the time for this. The decision who presents their solutions will be made partly on a voluntary basis, partly by chance and partly through my call. Therefore, it is important that you prepare the exercises for every session and are ready to present your results at any time. Should you be caught unprepared, I will consider this an unexcused absence.

In this module, we differentiate between simple exercises and exercises that have to be submitted. The latter are marked with an ⭐. Both, in the weekly briefing, and on the website. For the mandatory exercises, you upload your results via the OSCA submission function before each Monday session. The exact form will be specified in the assignment.

Content

The following list gives an overview of questions that we are going to address in the course of the semester:

Concepts

◼ What is information? What is an information system?
◼ What are typical tasks in information management?
◼ Why should we and how can we model information?
◼ What is a database? What types exist?
◼ What is an API and how can use it to automate information related tasks?

Tools

◼ What can and can't we do with Spreadsheets?
◼ How can we manage data with databases and SQL?
◼ How does a system like Shopify manage complex processes and information?
◼ How can we leverage Python and APIs to automate tasks?
